Step 3: The Four Futures

Detailed narratives of how the world could look in 2030.

A1 + B1

The Velvet Cage

The World Story

By 2030, the UAE finds itself in a peculiar position. Oil revenues have declined, forcing aggressive diversification. Yet regulators remain deeply conservative, viewing global fintech failures as validation of their cautious approach. Local markets feel trapped: clients want digital, but regulations make it unviable.

Early Warning Signposts
CBUAE issues licensing framework with AED 500M capital requirement.
Oil prices stay below $65 for three consecutive years.
A1 + B2

The Gilded Souk

The World Story

The 2020s close with oil at $110/barrel. UAE wealth swells, and a new generation of oil-services entrepreneurs creates a booming upper-middle class. Regulators stay traditional because the old model "just works." Relationships still determine access to the best deals.

Early Warning Signposts
Brent crude sustains above $95/barrel for 18 months.
HNW client surveys rank "personal relationship" as top priority.

The Digital Majlis

The World Story

A dramatic regulatory pivot occurs. With oil under pressure, the UAE embraces financial services as a pillar. Digital wealth licenses are fast-tracked. This triggers a Cambrian explosion of international platforms like Revolut and eToro entering the market.

Early Warning Signposts
Streamlined licensing with 90-day approval timelines announced.
Three international digital platforms receive licenses within 12 months.

The Golden Gateway

The World Story

The UAE dream scenario. Oil is high, and Singapore-style reforms make the UAE the "Wealth Hub of the East." Both HNW and mass-affluent classes explode. Winners are those who integrate high-touch service with a premium digital core.

Early Warning Signposts
UAE mass-affluent segment grows by 20% annually.
AlWasl digital waitlist exceeds 5,000 clients in 6 months.
